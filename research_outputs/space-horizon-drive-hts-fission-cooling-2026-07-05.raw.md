## Honest gaps

- **No net-power fusion, aneutronic or otherwise, exists** — search confirms Zap Energy is a D-T/D-D sheared-flow Z-pinch (neutronic, not aneutronic); aneutronic p-B11 (TAE/HB11) needs ~600 keV and has no net-gain device. Fusion is not a citable space-power source; the honest comparison is fission microreactor (real, ~6.7 W/kg, flight ~early 2030s) vs fusion (nonexistent). Framed as a documented absence, not a supported row.
- **QI/horizon-drive as space propulsion** — McCulloch frames QI as a space "horizon drive," but there is NO peer-reviewed space thrust demonstration; the physics remains unconfirmed (grounded elsewhere: `quantised-inertia-…`, `qi-haisch-rueda-puthoff-…`). This run grounds the ENABLING subsystems (power/cooling/HTS), not the drive itself.
- **HTS RF cavity Q at exactly 50 K in a full accelerating structure** — arXiv shows sample-level REBCO beating Cu but "still lower than niobium"; a full-cavity Q at 50 K matching Nb's 10¹⁰ is NOT demonstrated (historical high-Tc coated cavities "unsatisfactory"). The Q-vs-temperature tradeoff is real and unresolved.
- **Passive cooling below ~40 K** — JWST reaches 40 K passive but MIRI's 7 K needs an active cryocooler; so a 4 K Nb cavity would STILL need active cooling even in space (passive only removes the 300 K→40 K lift, not the 40 K→4 K lift).
